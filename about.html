about avishi

The story behind the study desk

A 17-year-old Indian student who started filming her study sessions and, somewhere between exam seasons and city moves, accidentally built a community.

who i am

Hi, I'm Avishi.

I'm a student who loves romanticising the ordinary parts of studying — the coffee, the color-coded notes, the 2 a.m. panic and the quiet wins in between. I started my channel because I couldn't find content that felt honest about what being a student actually feels like, so I decided to make it myself.

What began as a personal record of my own study habits slowly grew into a shared space — a place for students to feel less alone in the process, whether that's exam stress, moving away from home, or just figuring out who you are outside of a report card.

  • Based in India, studying while building a creator career alongside it
  • Content rooted in productivity, education, and everyday college life
  • Active across YouTube, Instagram, and LinkedIn with a consistent voice
the journey so far

How it started

A rough sketch of the milestones that shaped the brand — built from what's publicly shared across her platforms and interviews.

the beginning

First upload, first "study with me"

Avishi began posting study vlogs as a way to stay accountable to her own routine, filming sessions that were more diary entry than tutorial.

finding a voice

Content shifts from tips to storytelling

Rather than just productivity advice, the channel leaned into narrative — vlogs about hostel life, moving cities, and the emotional side of being a student.

going cross-platform

Instagram and LinkedIn join the mix

Instagram became the place for daily-life snapshots and community, while LinkedIn opened up a more professional conversation around personal branding and student entrepreneurship.

today

Building a genuine educational brand

What started as a personal habit tracker is now a recognisable, trusted voice for students navigating productivity, identity, and everything in between.

what i believe in

The values behind the content

Honesty over highlight reels

Showing the unfinished, unglamorous parts of student life — not just the aesthetic bits.

Education should feel human

Productivity content that respects that students are people first, not just GPAs to optimise.

Community over follower count

Genuine conversation in the comments matters more than any single metric.

Consistency, not perfection

Showing up regularly, even when a video isn't perfectly polished, builds more trust than chasing virality.
